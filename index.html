Project Overview

Quantum PvP

Quantum PvP is a game that starts with a quantum circuit with 3 qubits in superposition. 2 players take turns applying quantum gates onto the circuit, trying to manipulate the qubits, such that they collapse in a specific state. Players have 3 turns each and may add [X], [Y], [Z], [Rx], [Ry], [Rz], [CNOT], or the [SWAP] gate onto the circuit.

Project Overview

Quantum Machine Learning with QAOA

Last week, I was given an amazing opportunity to participate in a quantum computing hackathon. With amazing mentors from QOSF (The Quantum Open Source Foundation), my team used Rigetti's quantum computer and created a quantum machine learning program that clustered flowers based on their attributes. We then compared our program to classical machine learning and was amazed by the results.


Using the k-means clustering algorithm , we were able to achieve 100% accuracy in clustering 8 data samples in a time of 35.7 ms on a local CPU. With Rigetti's Quantum Optimization Approximation Algorithm (QAOA) and a weighted MaxCut clustering technique, we were able to achieve 100% accuracy in clustering 8 data samples in a time of 92,000 ms on Rigetti's Quantum Virtual Machine.

Rogers Continuous

With technology becoming ever more present in our day to day lives, it’s inevitable that people will run into technical difficulties. The current solutions of calling customer support and having someone visit you is both time consuming for customers and costly for companies. What if we could provide in-home support some other way?


By using augmented reality, we created an app that guides users through a step by step process of how to fix their technical problems. By scanning a QR code, our app will both diagnose and provide technical support. Users can see a virtual version of their hardware and find directions of how to solve the issue at hand.
